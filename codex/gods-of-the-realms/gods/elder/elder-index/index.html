Elder Gods

“They were not born of belief. They are what made belief possible.”
— Thalem Veydrin, Apocrypha of Flame, Cycle 12.7

Overview

Elder Gods are the oldest known divine entities in the world of The Casted Kin — pre-Exile, often pre-memory, and vastly beyond what most shrines can safely channel.

They were not ascended from Kynari or shaped by faith.
They shaped faith itself.

Most Elder Gods have fixed domains, represent universal forces (e.g. fire, recursion, silence), and possess a cultural permanence that persists even across collapses in belief. Their Ky conduits are woven deep into the Rootglass itself.

Even when forgotten, their shrines remain active.
Even when banished, they are not erased.

Traits of Elder Gods

TraitDescription
AgeAlways Pre-Exile — often thousands of cycles older than Solrael itself.
Domain FixationEach embodies a core element or law — flame, memory, void, time, recursion.
Shrine InstabilityElder God shrines can malfunction under weak offerings or misaligned Ky.
Divine InflexibilityCannot be bargained with or reinterpreted. Their rituals are absolute.
Memory AnchoringElder Gods resist forgetting. If erased, echoes persist in Rootglass dreams.
Refusal to EvolveThey do not change with Kynari beliefs — they demand the world conform to them.

“If a child draws them wrong, the shrine flares. If a song misnames them, the flame stops.”
— Vault-Incantor Ryllos, Cycle 87.2

Elder Gods tend to be polarizing — either central to a colony’s ideology or explicitly forbidden. Some vaults require their name be spoken backward. Some shrines remain active even when no one worships.

Worship in the Present Cycle

Elder Gods are rarely actively followed in most colonies due to:

  • Dangerous shrine behavior.
  • High Ky purity requirements.
  • Ritual inflexibility or taboo interactions.

However, some cults, pilgrims, or Surface Loyalist lineages maintain their worship in secrecy or tradition.

In gameplay terms:

  • Elder God shrines often offer high-reward, high-risk boons.
  • They may reject interaction if the player’s memory/Ky hue does not align.
  • Elder shrines may also react violently to polytheistic behavior.

Design Notes

  • Elder Gods provide moral anchors in the world — not necessarily “good,” but unchanging.
  • They can serve as antagonists, objectives, or mythic constants.
  • Unlike minor or ascended gods, Elder deities do not crave individual attention — they crave structure and completion of their law in the world.

“You do not pray to an Elder. You remember, correctly — or you are corrected.”
— Shrine Guardian proverb
