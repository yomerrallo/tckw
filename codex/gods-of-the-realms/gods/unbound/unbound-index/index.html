Unbound

“They are not gods, but gods begin to flinch when they speak.”
— Archive Note, blacklisted by the Shrine Consortium, Cycle 273.9

Overview

Unbound are beings who have transcended mortal limits — but have not yet ascended into the divine lattice. They are too powerful to remain Kynari, too unstable to be gods.

They are forbidden, feared, and often erased from shrine records.

Some are created by accident.
Others by divine manipulation.
A few walk the Depths with purpose: to become something more.

Unlike Ascended Gods (who are sanctified through shrines and worship), the Unbound exist outside the divine economy — no shrine accepts them, and yet their symbols appear, and their followers gather.

They are still mortal. But they are no longer bound by what mortality means.

TraitDescription

“If an Unbound makes a shrine, it isn’t worship. It’s a warning.”
— Whisper-Record, Cycle 251.1

Worship in the Present Cycle

Worship of the Unbound is unofficial, often covert, and always punishable by recognized god orders.

Still, their symbols appear in colony alleys, black-market ledgers, or carved deep into walls where no shrine dares follow.

Some colonists turn to the Unbound because:

  • No god has answered.
  • They desire personal power over communal safety.
  • They hate what the gods have become.

In gameplay terms:

  • Unbound entities offer unique buffs, equipment, and hidden interactions.
  • Aligning with one may lock out traditional shrines.
  • They may appear in dreams, visions, or during moments of trauma.

Design Notes

  • Unbound are the bridge between power and godhood — tempting players to pursue forbidden paths.
  • They act as wildcards in divine politics: neither sanctioned nor entirely rogue.
  • Some will ascend, others will fall, and a few may rewrite the laws of offering entirely.

“We cast them out because we knew what they could become — and what we’d have to kneel to.”
— Pilgrim testimony, denied entry into the Echo Vault
